Our most recent success is the Incubation of Indian Operations of Orzota Inc, a Big Data Startup in California.

Orzota contacted Radien in 2012, and was chartered with building out their product. Radien identified appropriate talent and presented to Orzota. Both building out the product and the team was a big challenge as given every startup's roadmap is fluid. After building out the team and product, Radien identified the managerial talent that could lead the team in India after the transfer. In February 2015 (After 2.5 years), Radien transferred the assets (IP, Code, and Engineers) to the new company and exited. This is the deceptively simple but challenging model of building out from scratch, growing and operating, and finding the appropriate time to transfer to the new company. As Radien manages everything in India, the client Orzota just has focuses on everything in USA. Everything else in India is taken care of by Radien. 

It is always an emotional event to bid good bye to a successful incubation, but these projects are so intense that it takes a toll on me, and I need to recover by taking some time off after multiyear incubation of a startup's India operation.

This is like a running a server in the cloud. You choose me/Radien. You rent the team. And then you build the product. Once you have established your product and basic sales, and you float a new company and take the assets that were built out during the incubation. Usually this takes about 3 years.

This is a good model for unfunded garage style startups who do not have a lot of cash or are on a tight budget (who is not?).

I have been doing this since 2007 for various innovators (primarily in USA). I would be happy to listen to your idea and discuss how to take your idea and make it a great product, backed with an "always on" responsive, talented team, that I run for you in India. You take the assets whenever you want.

BUILD -> OPERATE -> TRANSFER!!!
