Publications

Nonintrusive TCP Connection Admission Control for Bandwidth Management of an Internet Access
Link
IEEE Communications
Authors: Dinesh Thirumurthy, Anand Svr, Arzad Alam Kherani
Abstract:
We describe our approach to monitoring and managing the bandwidth of an Internet edge link with a view towards certain quality of service objectives for the services it carries. Such a link could be, for example, a campus’s Internet access link, or a small ISP’s backbone access link. We use SNMP polls and packet snooping to obtain traffic statistics, and TCP admission control for bandwidth management. Our implementation is completely nonintrusive: we use Ethernet packet capture in the promiscuous mode for traffic analysis, and use IP masquerading for blocking new TCP connections.
This approach has been implemented by us in a software system for traffic management. We first justify our approach with a simple analytical model.
We give an overview of our software implementation, and discuss some implementation
issues. Then we provide measurement results that show the effectiveness of the techniques.
